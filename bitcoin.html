Bitcoin — The First Cryptocurrency

Bitcoin is the world’s first decentralized digital currency, created in 2009 by an anonymous figure known as Satoshi Nakamoto. It introduced the concept of a peer‑to‑peer financial system without banks, governments, or intermediaries.

How Bitcoin Works

Bitcoin operates on a public ledger called the blockchain. Every transaction is recorded, verified by thousands of computers worldwide, and secured through cryptography. This makes Bitcoin transparent, resistant to censorship, and extremely difficult to manipulate.

Why Bitcoin Matters

Bitcoin is often called “digital gold” because it has a limited supply of 21 million coins. This scarcity, combined with decentralization, makes it attractive as a store of value and a hedge against inflation.

How People Use Bitcoin

• As an investment • As a method of payment • As a way to transfer money globally • As a long‑term store of value

Risks to Consider

Bitcoin is volatile, meaning its price can rise or fall quickly. It also requires secure storage, usually through hardware wallets or trusted custodial services.
