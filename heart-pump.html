The Heart as a Pump

The heart is a strong, rhythmic muscular pump responsible for circulating blood throughout the body. With every beat, it delivers oxygen and nutrients to organs and tissues, while removing carbon dioxide and metabolic waste.

The Four Chambers

The heart is divided into four chambers that work together in a coordinated cycle:

• Right atrium — receives deoxygenated blood from the body • Right ventricle — pumps blood to the lungs for oxygenation • Left atrium — receives oxygen-rich blood from the lungs • Left ventricle — pumps oxygenated blood to the entire body

The Cardiac Cycle

The cardiac cycle consists of two main phases:

• Systole — the heart contracts and pumps blood forward • Diastole — the heart relaxes and fills with blood

This cycle repeats about 60–100 times per minute in a healthy adult.

Why This Matters

Understanding the heart as a pump helps explain many cardiovascular conditions. When the pump weakens or becomes inefficient, symptoms such as fatigue, swelling, dizziness, or shortness of breath may appear.

How to Support a Healthy Heart

• Regular physical activity • Balanced diet rich in fruits and vegetables • Avoiding smoking • Managing stress • Monitoring blood pressure • Maintaining a healthy weight
