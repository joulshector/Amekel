Blockchain — How It Works

Blockchain is a decentralized digital ledger that records transactions across thousands of computers worldwide. Instead of relying on a central authority, the blockchain ensures transparency, security, and immutability through cryptography.

The Structure of a Blockchain

A blockchain is made up of blocks, and each block contains:

• A list of transactions • A timestamp • A cryptographic hash • The hash of the previous block

This creates a chain of blocks that cannot be altered without changing every block that comes after it.

Why Blockchain Is Secure

Blockchain security comes from decentralization. Instead of storing data in one place, the ledger is distributed across thousands of nodes. Any attempt to alter the data would require controlling more than half of the network — something extremely difficult and expensive.

Consensus Mechanisms

To validate transactions, blockchains use consensus algorithms such as:

• Proof of Work (PoW) — used by Bitcoin • Proof of Stake (PoS) — used by many modern blockchains

These mechanisms ensure that all participants agree on the state of the ledger.

Real‑World Applications

Blockchain technology is used in:

• Cryptocurrencies • Supply chain tracking • Digital identity • Smart contracts • Voting systems

Why Blockchain Matters

Blockchain represents a shift from centralized control to decentralized trust. It enables secure, transparent, and tamper‑resistant systems that can transform industries.
